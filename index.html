Featured Cases

Junko Furuta

The horrifying murder of a Japanese high school student in 1989.

Junko Furuta was a bright, ambitious 17-year-old when she was abducted by a group of young men in Tokyo. She endured 44 days of unspeakable torture before her captors ultimately killed her and disposed of her body in a barrel of concrete. The case shocked Japan and raised conversations about juvenile justice and the need for reform.

Elizabeth Short (Black Dahlia)

The infamous unsolved murder that shocked Los Angeles in 1947.

Elizabeth Short, a 22-year-old aspiring actress, was found mutilated in Los Angeles in 1947. Her body had been gruesomely bisected, and the crime scene was eerily staged. Despite numerous suspects, the case remains unsolved, fueling speculation and conspiracy theories.

Leonarda Cianciulli (The Soap-Maker of Correggio)

An Italian woman who turned her victims into soap and tea cakes.

Leonarda Cianciulli, an Italian woman from the 1930s, believed she needed human sacrifices to protect her son. She murdered three women, dismembered their bodies, and horrifyingly used the remains to create soap and tea cakes. Her case shocked Italy, blending elements of superstition, desperation, and gruesome reality.
